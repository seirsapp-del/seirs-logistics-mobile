## Quick verdict

The current SEIRS Terms and Privacy Policy are a credible **starter draft** —
well-structured, plain-English, and hit the obvious bases (eligibility,
prohibited items, refunds, data subject rights, NDPC complaint route).
However, they are **not launch-ready for a regulated wallet/PII processor
in Nigeria**. Major gaps: no CAMA corporate disclosures, no NDPA Section 32
DPO designation, no 72-hour breach notification clause, no CBN/AML wallet
language, no FCCPA-compliant consumer dispute pathway, no NIN/BVN-specific
lawful basis, and the Terms reference Paystack while the spec uses
Flutterwave (factual mismatch). A Nigerian lawyer review is mandatory.

## Terms of Service gaps

- **No CAMA 2020 corporate disclosure** — must list registered company
  name, RC number, registered office address, and (if applicable) any
  sector licence numbers.
- **Payment processor mismatch** — Section 6 names Paystack; spec uses
  Flutterwave. Fix in both Terms and Privacy.
- **No FCCPA Section 124 alignment on unfair contract terms** — the
  unilateral 7-day pricing change clause and "with or without notice"
  suspension clause are likely unenforceable as drafted.
- **No FCCPA-compliant consumer complaints/escalation path** — needs
  tiered process (in-app support → formal complaint → FCCPA referral)
  with response SLAs.
- **Three-account-type asymmetry not addressed** — Terms mix customer,
  business, driver, and partner-store obligations in one document.
  Driver terms in particular need a separate independent-contractor
  schedule.
- **No driver deactivation / appeal procedure** — Section 9 allows
  termination "with or without notice"; under FCCPA and gig-worker
  norms, drivers should get a stated appeal window.
- **No standalone force majeure clause** — needs Nigeria-specific
  coverage (EFCC freezes, CBN directives, fuel scarcity, NCC outages,
  civil unrest).
- **₦50,000 liability cap unsupported by tier** — should reference
  declared-value tiers, insurance availability, and exclusions.
- **No e-signature / electronic contract clause** — should expressly
  invoke the Evidence Act 2011 (s.84) to make in-app acceptance binding.
- **No anti-money-laundering / sanctions clause** — required because
  of the wallet (see fintech section).
- **No third-party beneficiary disclaimer** — recipients are not parties
  to the contract; needs stating.
- **No insurance disclosure** — does SEIRS provide goods-in-transit
  insurance? Currently silent.
- **Arbitration clause underspecified** — needs number of arbitrators,
  language, institutional vs ad hoc, and a **carve-out for small claims**
  (FCCPA gives consumers non-waivable right to FCCPC and small claims
  courts).
- Missing standard clauses: severability, assignment / change of control.
- **No promotional T&Cs hook** — wallet promos, referral codes, loyalty
  points all need a "subject to specific promo terms" hook.
- **Cash-handling for drivers not addressed** — driver app supports cash
  payouts; Terms must cover driver liability for cash-on-delivery.

## Privacy Policy gaps

- **No DPO designation or contact** — NDPA Section 32 requires a Data
  Protection Officer for organisations processing personal data of more
  than 2,000 data subjects in 6 months (you'll exceed this easily).
  `privacy@seirs.co` is not a named DPO.
- **No NDPC registration / Data Controller of Major Importance (DCMI)
  statement** — under NDPA and the GAID 2025, organisations meeting
  thresholds must register with NDPC.
- **No 72-hour breach notification commitment** — NDPA Section 40
  requires controllers to notify NDPC within 72 hours.
- **No NIN / BVN-specific clause** — KYC will use NIN and BVN. These
  attract heightened obligations under NIMC Act, BVN Operations
  Guidelines, and NDPA "sensitive personal data" treatment.
- **Sensitive personal data category missing** — NDPA Section 30 requires
  explicit consent for sensitive data (biometric, ID document images).
- **Lawful bases too thin** — NDPA Section 25 lists six bases. Policy
  lists four; missing **vital interests** and **public interest**, and
  doesn't map each processing purpose to a specific basis.
- **Data subject rights list incomplete vs NDPA Sections 34–43** —
  missing **right to lodge complaint with NDPC** in the rights list,
  **right not to be subject to automated decision-making** (Section 37),
  and **right to restrict processing**.
- **Cross-border transfer clause too vague** — NDPA Sections 41–43
  require either an adequacy decision, BCRs, SCCs, or explicit consent.
  Currently mentions "EU and US" — verify against actual Railway region
  and the lawful mechanism per recipient.
- **No third-party processor list (Article 30 register-style)** —
  Flutterwave, Google Maps, Firebase, Railway, SMS gateway, email
  provider should each be named with purpose, location, policy link.
- **Children's privacy weak** — NDPA Section 31 sets the digital consent
  age at 13 with parental consent up to 18.
- **Retention table needs legal grounding** — "7 years" should cite
  CBN/AML record-keeping rules; "90 days" GPS retention should cite
  legitimate-interest balancing.
- **Marketing consent mechanism unspecified** — opt-in/withdrawal +
  NCC do-not-disturb (DND) compliance for SMS not stated.
- **No automated decision-making / profiling disclosure** — fraud
  detection, driver matching, dynamic pricing all qualify.
- **Cookies section thin** — analytics on the marketing website (GA,
  Plausible, Meta Pixel) must be disclosed if used.
- **No CCTV / call-recording clause** — if support calls are recorded.

## Wallet / fintech-specific clauses needed

Because of the Flutterwave-funded wallet + driver cash earnings, SEIRS
must add (whether or not it holds a CBN licence itself, since it
operates a stored-value wallet on top of a licensed PSSP):

- **CBN tiered KYC disclosure** — Tier 1/2/3 limits, document
  requirements, max balance, max single transfer.
- **AML/CFT clause** — Money Laundering (Prevention and Prohibition)
  Act 2022 + Terrorism (Prevention and Prohibition) Act 2022; right
  to freeze, report to NFIU, request source-of-funds.
- **Customer fund safeguarding statement** — wallet balances held in
  designated trust/escrow account at a CBN-licensed bank, segregated
  from operating funds. (Single biggest consumer-trust clause.)
- **Sanctions screening** — OFAC, UN, EU, NFIU PEP lists.
- **Chargeback & dispute handling** — timeframes, evidence required,
  Flutterwave's role, customer escalation to CBN Consumer Protection.
- **Dormant wallet policy** — CBN dormant account guidelines (6 months
  → dormant; 10 years → unclaimed funds to CBN).
- **Unauthorised transaction liability allocation** — SIM-swap, OTP
  compromise, device theft. CBN expects clarity.
- **Transaction limits and right to vary them** — daily, monthly caps.
- **Driver cash-handling clause** — driver acts as agent for cash
  collected, must remit, settlement timeline, shortage liability.
- **Cybercrimes Act 2015 (as amended 2024) acknowledgement** —
  fraud, identity theft, unauthorised access prosecutable under
  Sections 6, 13, 14, 22.
- **Risk-Based Cybersecurity Framework alignment** — short statement
  that SEIRS aligns to CBN's RBCF and ISO 27001 principles.

## Competitor patterns to consider adopting

> Verify clauses directly before lifting. WebFetch was unable to pull
> the live URLs in this audit; patterns below are from prior knowledge.

**Bolt Nigeria (passenger terms)**
- Two-document split: Platform T&Cs and separate Driver Agreement.
- Strong independent-contractor recital for drivers + deactivation
  appeals clause with stated review window.
- Three-tier dispute escalation: in-app → email support → arbitration,
  with consumer carve-out for FCCPC.

**Flutterwave Nigeria**
- Explicit CBN PSSP/Switching licence number disclosure at the top.
- Detailed chargeback/dispute timeline table.
- AML clause naming Money Laundering Act 2022 + right to request
  source-of-funds documentation.

**Kuda**
- Customer-fund safeguarding statement naming the trust bank — gold
  standard for wallet trust language.
- Tiered KYC table with limits.
- Dormancy clause with CBN-aligned 6-month / 10-year escalation.

**Uber Nigeria privacy notice**
- Per-purpose lawful-basis mapping table (addresses NDPA Section 25
  granularity gap directly).
- Detailed automated decision-making / driver-matching disclosure with
  right to human review.
- Clear retention table by data category with legal basis cited per row.

**OPay Nigeria**
- CBN MMO licence disclosure + consumer-protection complaint route at
  bottom of every legal page.
- Unauthorised transaction liability matrix allocating fault between
  OPay, customer, and bank.
- Strong prohibited transactions list for fintech (gambling, crypto,
  MLM, forex hedging).
