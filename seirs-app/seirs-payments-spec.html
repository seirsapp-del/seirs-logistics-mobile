① Principles & Regulatory Context

The non-negotiable rules every other section flows from.

SEIRS does not hold customer money. CBN regulations require any entity that holds customer balances to be a licensed bank, microfinance bank, or PSP (Payment Service Provider) under MMO/PSSP/PSB licence. SEIRS holds none of these, so we use a pass-through model: every Naira flows through Flutterwave directly to either the SEIRS company bank account (revenue) or the driver's bank account (payout). No customer-funded "wallet" balances live on our servers.

Hard rules

RuleWhy
No customer money is stored on SEIRS servers.Avoids CBN PSP licence requirement. All charges are pass-through via Flutterwave.
Customer-side "balance" = Loyalty Points, not money.McDonald's-style points have no monetary face value; redeemable only for SEIRS services. Not regulated as e-money.
Driver "earnings" = ledger entry, not held cash.SEIRS records what the driver is owed; Flutterwave Transfers API is the mechanism that moves real money from SEIRS bank → driver bank on payout day.
One payment processor: Flutterwave.Single relationship simplifies reconciliation, dispute handling, and contractual liability. Paystack and other processors removed.
Refunds always go to the original payment method.Anti-money-laundering (AML) — money out must match money in. Flutterwave's refund API enforces this by default.
Dispute window before driver payout: REQUIREDCustomer must have a 24-72h window to flag issues (damaged goods, no-show) before the driver's earnings move from "pending" to "paid out".

What this means in plain language

  • When a customer pays for a delivery, the money goes Flutterwave → SEIRS bank. SEIRS now owes the driver their share.
  • SEIRS books the driver's share as a liability ("driver earnings owed"). This sits in our internal ledger, not in a real wallet.
  • On the driver's payout day, SEIRS triggers a Flutterwave Transfer from our bank account to the driver's bank account. The liability clears.
  • Loyalty points are awarded for usage, redeemable only for SEIRS service discounts. They have no cash value, cannot be withdrawn, and are not transferable.

② Money Flow Architecture

How a Naira moves from a customer's pocket to a driver's bank — fully traceable, no SEIRS-held funds in transit.

Customer payment flow (per delivery)

Customer taps "Pay" in the app │ ▼ Flutterwave Inline (web view) — customer enters card / chooses USSD / bank transfer / Opay │ ▼ Flutterwave processes payment + holds in SEIRS merchant account │ ▼ Webhook → SEIRS backend (POST /webhooks/flutterwave) │ ├─→ Mark Delivery as paid ├─→ Credit driver's earnings ledger (status: "pending") ├─→ Award customer loyalty points └─→ Push notification: "Driver assigned, on the way" │ ▼ T+1 settlement: Flutterwave deposits net amount to SEIRS Nigerian bank account │ ▼ Reconciliation job matches settlement to deliveries (daily cron)

Driver payout flow

Delivery completed + dispute window expires (e.g. 24h) │ ▼ Driver earnings status: "pending" → "available" │ ▼ Driver requests payout (or auto-payout cadence triggers — daily/weekly) │ ▼ SEIRS backend calls Flutterwave Transfers API (POST /v3/transfers — from SEIRS bank to driver's verified bank account) │ ▼ Flutterwave debits SEIRS account, credits driver's account │ ▼ Webhook → SEIRS backend updates ledger status: "available" → "paid" │ ▼ Push + email to driver: "₦X paid out to your GTBank account"

Reconciliation

A nightly cron job pulls Flutterwave's settlement report and matches each settled transaction to a Delivery row. Discrepancies (missing settlements, fee mismatches) are flagged in the admin dashboard for manual review.

⑤ Loyalty Points System (McDonald's-style)

Customer-facing rewards that drive repeat usage WITHOUT putting SEIRS into e-money territory.

Why points, not wallet balance: Points have no NGN face value. They are a contractual reward earned by usage, redeemable only for SEIRS service discounts. They cannot be withdrawn, transferred, or sold. CBN does not regulate this — same legal status as airline miles or hotel points.

Payout method

Flutterwave Transfers API. Driver provides bank account during onboarding (KYC step). SEIRS verifies via Flutterwave's bank-name-resolution endpoint before allowing payout. Each Transfer creates a webhook callback so we can confirm success or failure.

Refund mechanism

Refunds are issued via Flutterwave's POST /v3/transactions/:id/refund endpoint. Money returns to the original payment method (card refund, bank account, etc.) within Flutterwave's stated timelines (1-7 business days depending on method). The matching loyalty points are also clawed back.

⑧ Fraud Protection (multi-layer)

Fraud comes from three sides: customers, drivers, and customer-driver collusion. Defense in depth.

Customer-side fraud

RiskDefense
Stolen card used for delivery → chargeback laterFlutterwave's risk score on every transaction. Trigger 3DS for high-risk. Block accounts with ≥2 chargebacks.
Fake delivery for refund (claim "didn't arrive")Driver photo proof at delivery. GPS trail. Recipient signature/OTP at handoff (already in V8 spec).
Coupon abuse (one user, many accounts)Device fingerprinting. Phone number verification. Block referral if same device/IP/payment method.
Loyalty point farmingAnti-abuse: max 10 referrals/month, can't refer same person twice, points only awarded after referee's 1st paid order.

Driver-side fraud

RiskDefense
Driver steals packagePickup photo + recipient OTP at delivery (V8 §1.17). Hold of earnings until customer confirms receipt. Mandatory KYC + government ID + bank account verification.
Cash-on-delivery driver pockets cashReal-time settlement: driver must confirm cash collection in app, owes SEIRS that amount, deducted from next payout. Negative balance = account suspension + collections.
Fake delivery completions ("ghost rides")GPS verification: driver must be within 100m of pickup AND dropoff to mark complete. Customer must confirm delivery (push tap or OTP).
Driver creates fake customer account to game incentivesSame device/payment cross-reference. Block earnings until manual review.

RiskDefense

⑨ Company Protection (legal & operational)

Things that need to be in your Terms of Service, your operational playbook, and your insurance contracts.

Terms of Service must-haves

  • Pass-through model disclosure: SEIRS is a logistics matchmaker, not a financial institution. We do not hold customer funds. All payments are processed by Flutterwave under Flutterwave's terms.
  • Liability cap: SEIRS' maximum liability for any single delivery is the lower of (a) the value declared by the customer at booking and (b) the insurance cap (e.g. ₦100,000 standard, optional upgrade).
  • Arbitration clause: Disputes resolved via arbitration in Lagos under Nigerian Arbitration Act, not court. Reduces legal cost exposure.
  • Driver as independent contractor: Drivers are not employees. They use the SEIRS platform but are not on payroll, not entitled to benefits. Critical for cost structure and labour-law exposure.
  • Right to refuse service: SEIRS may refuse any delivery (illegal goods, restricted items, fraud risk).
  • Loyalty points: Have no cash value, may be modified or revoked at SEIRS' discretion, expire after 24 months of inactivity.
  • Data & KYC: Customer/driver consent to identity verification, background checks (drivers), photo capture at delivery.

⑩ Revenue Streams & Profit Maximization

Multiple revenue lines reduce dependence on any single one. Build all of them but prioritize 1–3.

Margin-stack philosophy: Per-delivery commission alone is thin (~20-25% net of Flutterwave fees + driver share). Layer recurring + ad-hoc revenue on top to multiply contribution per active user. Industry benchmark: top logistics apps generate 1.6×–2.2× per-delivery revenue from non-commission streams.

Cost levers to watch

  • Flutterwave fee negotiation: Default rates assume low volume. Once volume hits ₦50M/month+, negotiate down to ~0.9% on local cards.
  • Driver acquisition cost (CAC): Cap at ₦10,000 per active driver. Refer-a-driver bonus is cheaper than ads.
  • Customer support: Use AI chatbot for L1 (FAQ, tracking), human for L2 (disputes). Saves ~70% on support cost.
  • Reverse logistics: Returns are expensive — discourage unless paid for, or batch return-pickups.

⑪ Implementation Plan (engineering)

Phased build. Phase 1 unblocks v1 launch; phases 2-3 are post-launch.

Phase 1 — v1 launch (must-have, ~2-3 weeks)

  1. Backend: PaymentMethod entity (token, last4, expiry, isDefault) — stores Flutterwave card tokens only.
  2. Backend: FlutterwaveService abstraction (charge, tokenize, refund, transfer).
  3. Backend: POST /webhooks/flutterwave handler — verify HMAC, mark deliveries paid, credit driver ledger.
  4. Backend: DriverEarning ledger entity (deliveryId, amount, status: pending|available|paid|held).
  5. Backend: cron job — flip pending → available after dispute window.
  6. Backend: cron job — daily payout via Flutterwave Transfers API.
  7. Backend: LoyaltyPoint ledger entity — earn on payment webhook, redeem at booking.
  8. Customer app: replace mock cards with real GET /payment-methods, add card via Flutterwave Inline.
  9. Customer app: payment method picker at checkout (cards + bank transfer + USSD).
  10. Business app: copy customer-app's payment-methods UI into business; same flow but tied to business account.
  11. Driver app: earnings dashboard (today, this week, all-time) + payout history + bank-account verification.
  12. Admin dashboard: reconciliation report, manual refund tool, driver payout queue, fraud-flagged accounts.
  13. Remove all Paystack references — Flutterwave is the only processor.
  14. Hide mock-cards UI in customer-app immediately (until real backend wires up).

Phase 2 — v1.1 (post-launch, ~3-4 weeks)

  1. SEIRS Plus subscription (consumer) — Flutterwave recurring billing.
  2. Surge pricing engine — based on demand/supply by zone.
  3. Loyalty tier upgrades + automated tier emails.
  4. Insurance integration (partner with AIICO/Leadway).
  5. Pay-on-delivery flow with driver settlement.
  6. Velocity limits + automated fraud-flagging engine.

Phase 3 — v1.2 (~6+ weeks out)

  1. Driver Pro subscription.
  2. White-label API.
  3. In-app ad placements.
  4. Data analytics product for B2B.

Pre-launch checklist (legal/ops)

  • Get a payments lawyer to review T&C — confirm CBN positioning is sound.
  • Procure insurance products (in-transit, driver accident, cyber liability).
  • Register with NITDA (NDPR compliance) — assign DPO.
  • Negotiate Flutterwave volume rate down before scaling.
  • Confirm state operator licences for Lagos + initial expansion cities.
  • Publish dispute resolution playbook + complaint email/phone.
